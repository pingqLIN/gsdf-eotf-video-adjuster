# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Developers and advanced visual-tuning users who inspect web video directly inside Chrome. They use the extension while evaluating grayscale separation, perceptual contrast, gamma compensation, and luminance behavior on real video surfaces, often in iterative test-and-adjust loops rather than one-time setup flows.

## Product Purpose

GSDF EOTF Video Adjuster is a task-focused control surface for display-side perceptual tuning. It helps users reshape visible grayscale behavior, compare output-preview stripes, inspect calibration-oriented patterns, and validate tone-curve consequences without leaving the current video page. Success means the interface feels fast, trustworthy, and inspection-oriented, with enough precision for repeated visual testing while staying compact enough for everyday use.

## Brand Personality

Precise, observant, disciplined.

The interface should feel like a research-grade visual inspection tool with the confidence of a professional monitoring console. It should not feel playful, luxurious, or theatrical. It should support analytical focus, quick adjustment, and repeated comparison without overwhelming the user.

## Anti-references

- Do not make it feel like a gaming RGB control panel.
- Do not make it feel like a legacy medical-device UI.
- Do not make it feel like a generic SaaS admin dashboard.
- Do not over-index on decorative neon or glossy "cyberpunk" styling.
- Do not over-formalize it into a calibration instrument aesthetic that feels heavier than the task.

## Design Principles

- Keep the operator in flow: primary adjustments and verification views should feel immediate, not ceremonious.
- Make precision legible: typography, numbers, lines, and state cues should read clearly at a glance.
- Prefer controlled density over decoration: compact does not mean cramped, and detail should feel organized rather than loud.
- Separate inspection roles cleanly: live tuning, preview stripes, calibration references, and analytical overlays should each have a distinct visual job.
- Let interaction convey confidence: dragging, resizing, toggling, and opening overlays should feel direct and dependable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG AA contrast for core text and controls.
- Preserve visible focus states for keyboard users.
- Keep motion subtle and purposeful, and avoid decorative transitions that could interfere with inspection work.
- Ensure dense controls remain readable on common desktop displays without requiring oversized UI chrome.
